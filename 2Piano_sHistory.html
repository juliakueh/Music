The Foundation of the Piano

The innovation of early keyboard instruments laid the foundation for the birth of the piano. Centuries of using pipe organs and the earliest string instrument, the dulcimers, greatly contributed to the creation of the piano.Their existence to a large extent inspired the emergence of the piano (bnaturalPianoAdmin, 2020).

The first piano

Bartolomeo Cristofori is credited as the inventor of the piano. Cristofori opted to utilize a hammer instead of the harpsichord's plucking mechanism because he was dissatisfied with how the instrument controlled the volume level. He was successful in building a hammer-action keyboard. Around the year 1700, he is credited with developing the modern piano (Grand Piano, 2020).

Modern piano

In the late 1700s, the development of piano manufacturing led to the emergence of the modern piano we have today. Compared to the earliest pianos, modern pianos have undergone significant structural changes to produce a higher quality of sound. Three strings were added to each note to provide rich, complex, and beautiful sounds, given two more octaves for a total of seven octaves and repetition levers were added to make it possible to swiftly repeat the same note were the changed greatly appreciated by composers such as Chopin, Liszt, Debussy, and Ravel. These modern improvements marked the arrival of the Romantic era and the evolution of classical music (bnaturalPianoAdmin, 2020).
