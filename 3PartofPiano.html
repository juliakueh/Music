Pedals

Soft pedal(left) is designed to shift the keyboard to the right to causes the hammers to strike only two out of three piano strings, creating a slightly softer sound effect.

When pressing the sostenuto pedal(middle), it lifts a rod in the piano that holds one key's damper off the strings. This allowing that note to keep playing while other notes are not affected, until release the sostenuto pedal.

Sustain Pedal(right) own the function just sound as it's name. Pressing the sustain pedal lifts all the dampers, letting strings resonate after releasing a key (Li, 2022).

Soundboard

When a key is pressed, a hammer strikes the strings, initially creating a soft sound. The strings are attached to bridges that transmit their vibrations to the soundboard, making it vibrate and produce the loud sound that the entire piano generates (Yamaha,(n.d)).

Damper

The damper plays the role of controls the damping, or stopping of the string's vibration. Dampers can be found on most of the strings on the piano. The very high-pitched strings don't make as much sound as the longer ones and don't have dampers (smit, n.d.).

MusicStand

The music desk is where the pianist places their sheet music. It has a lower part called the music shelf that supports the bottom of the music. The desk can be adjusted to different angles and folded down when sheet music isn't used. (smit, n.d.).
